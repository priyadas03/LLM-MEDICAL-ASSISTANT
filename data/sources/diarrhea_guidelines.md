# Diarrhea (General Guidance)

Diarrhea often improves with time, hydration, and supportive care. The risk of complications depends on age, underlying health, and warning signs.

Seek urgent medical evaluation if diarrhea is accompanied by:
- signs of dehydration (very little urine, dizziness, severe weakness)
- blood in the stool
- severe belly pain
- persistent high fever

For non-emergency cases:
- Focus on hydration with small frequent sips.
- Consider oral rehydration solution if available.
- Eat light foods as tolerated.

If symptoms persist, worsen, or you are high risk (very young, older adult, immunocompromised), consider contacting a clinician.
