# Fever (General Guidance)

Fever is often caused by infections, but the severity of symptoms and associated warning signs matter more than the exact number on a thermometer.

Seek emergency care immediately if you have fever with any of the following:
- trouble breathing, severe weakness, chest pain, confusion, fainting
- stiff neck, new rash, or severe headache
- seizure

For non-emergency cases:
- Stay hydrated (small frequent sips of water or oral rehydration solution if tolerated).
- Rest.
- Consider checking temperature trends over time.
- If you are immunocompromised, have significant chronic illness, are very young, or symptoms are worsening, seek urgent medical evaluation.

When you contact a clinician, mention the duration of fever, the highest measured temperature, associated symptoms, and any relevant medical history.
