# Dehydration (General Guidance)

Dehydration can occur when the body loses more fluids than it takes in (for example, from vomiting, diarrhea, fever, or not drinking enough).

Seek emergency care if dehydration is severe or you notice:
- very little or no urine output
- extreme drowsiness, confusion, fainting
- trouble breathing
- inability to keep fluids down with worsening symptoms

For milder cases (if safe to do so):
- Take small sips of fluids regularly.
- If available, oral rehydration solutions may be more effective than plain water for some cases.
- Monitor for improvement over the next several hours.

If symptoms are worsening, or you have significant underlying health problems, consider urgent medical evaluation.
